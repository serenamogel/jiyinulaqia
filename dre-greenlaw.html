Dre Greenlaw’s Age, Biography

Dre Greenlaw was raised in group homes and shelters in Arkansas from the age of eight. of 14, he met Brian Early, the defensive coach of Fayetteville High School. Greenlaw was asked to live with the Early family, and at the age of 21, he was legally adopted.

Greenlaw attended Fayetteville Public Schools and played on the Fayetteville High School football team, which won the Class 7A state title in 2011. He was chosen to the Arkansas Democrat-Gazette’s preseason “Super Sophomore” team in 2012. Greenlaw started at cornerback for a squad that won the 7A state championship again, earning 53 total tackles, two forced fumbles, five pass breakups, and an interception. As a junior, he switched to strong safety.

Dre Greenlaw’s Profession

Dre Greenlaw was selected by the San Francisco 49ers in the fifth round (148th overall) in the 2019 NFL Draft. He was selected when the 49ers traded linebacker Dekoda Watson for the 148th selection.

Greenlaw had four tackles in his NFL debut, a 31-17 away win against the Tampa Bay Buccaneers. In a 28-25 road win against the Arizona Cardinals in Week 9, he produced four tackles and his first NFL sack of Kyler Murray. In the following Monday Night Football game against the Seattle Seahawks, Greenlaw had eight tackles, a pass deflection, and his first NFL interception off Russell Wilson in a 27-24 overtime defeat. Greenlaw collected ten tackles the next week against the Cardinals, helping the 49ers win 36–26.

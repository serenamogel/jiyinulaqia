Burt Reynolds: The Life and Legacy of a Hollywood Icon

What is Burt Reynolds’ Net Worth and Salary?

Burt Reynolds’ net worth at the time of his death in 2018 was estimated to be approximately $5 million. However, it’s important to note that Reynolds was once one of the highest-paid actors in Hollywood, earning as much as $10 million per movie in the 1970s and 1980s. Unfortunately, bad investments, a costly divorce, and financial mismanagement left Reynolds in significant debt by the time of his death.

Early Life and Education

Burt Reynolds was born in Waycross, Georgia on February 11, 1936. His father was a military police officer and his mother was a nurse. Reynolds attended Florida State University on a football scholarship, but a series of injuries forced him to quit the sport. He then turned to acting, studying at the Hyde Park Playhouse in New York City and the Pasadena Playhouse in California.

Career

Reynolds’ acting career began in the late 1950s with small roles in television shows such as Gunsmoke and Riverboat. He then moved on to film, earning critical acclaim for his performance in Deliverance in 1972. Reynolds became a leading man in Hollywood, starring in hit films such as Smokey and the Bandit, The Longest Yard, and Boogie Nights. In addition to acting, Reynolds also directed several films, including Gator and Sharky’s Machine.

Personal Life

Burt Reynolds was married twice, first to actress Judy Carne and later to Loni Anderson. Despite his numerous romantic relationships with high-profile women such as Dinah Shore and Sally Field, Reynolds often spoke about his regrets regarding his personal life. He struggled with addiction to painkillers and alcohol for much of his life and spent time in rehab facilities.

Illness and Death

Burt Reynolds passed away on September 6, 2018, at the age of 82. The official cause of death was a heart attack. In the years leading up to his death, Reynolds had several health issues, including heart bypass surgery and a severe case of the flu. Despite his health problems, Reynolds continued to work in film and television until his death.

Real Estate

Burt Reynolds was a real estate enthusiast and owned several properties throughout his life. One of his most notable properties was a 153-acre ranch in Jupiter, Florida, which he purchased in the early 1980s. The property features a 4,000-square-foot main house, a 2,748-square-foot guesthouse, and several outbuildings. Reynolds also owned several other properties in Florida, as well as a ranch in Arkansas.

Legacy

Burt Reynolds’ legacy in Hollywood is undeniable. He was a true icon of the silver screen, having appeared in more than 90 films and television shows throughout his career. Reynolds was beloved by fans and colleagues alike for his charismatic screen presence, his sense of humor, and his dedication to the craft of acting. His legacy lives on through his films and through the Burt Reynolds Institute for Film & Theatre in Jupiter, Florida.

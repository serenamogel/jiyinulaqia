Pete Carroll serves as an American football coach, currently holding the positions of head coach and executive vice president for the Seattle Seahawks in the National Football League (NFL). 

Before his tenure with the Seahawks, Carroll served as the head football coach at USC from 2001 to 2009, achieving notable success with six bowl game victories and a National Championship in 2004.

Although he experienced limited success with the New York Jets in 1994 and the New England Patriots from 1997 to 1999 during his early professional coaching career, Carroll transitioned to college football with USC.

There, he successfully transformed the struggling program into a highly-ranked contender. Under Carroll’s leadership, the Seahawks have made significant achievements.

Carroll has had an interesting personal life, particularly in terms of his marriages. His first marriage was to Wendy Pearl in 1973, and despite keeping their relationship details private, the couple divorced in 1975 after only two years of marriage. 

The specific reasons for their separation remain undisclosed, as Carroll and Wendy chose to keep their private matters away from the public eye. The ex-wife continued to stay away from the media and public attention and has deliberately maintained a low profile since the divorce.

After the brief marriage with Wendy, the football coach quickly remarried less than a year later. As per the source, this suggested that the NFL coach may have already been involved with his current wife during his divorce from Pearl. 

In contrast to her previous visibility during her marriage to the NFL coach, Pearl has chosen to stay out of the limelight, possibly to avoid the relentless scrutiny that often comes with being associated with a public figure.

Pete Carroll married his current wife, Glena Goranson, within the year of the divorce 

As for Carroll, he moved on from his first marriage and married Glena Goranson on May 21, 1976. The couple shares a connection through their shared background as college athletes. Born in the year 1955, she is 68 years old as of 2023.

Despite Carroll’s prominent position in the limelight, the couple has chosen to keep their family life discreet. Clutch Points mentioned that Goranson pursued her education at the University of the Pacific, the same college attended by Carroll. 

While Carroll was involved in football, Goranson played on the university’s volleyball team. The couple exchanged wedding vows in 1976, and since then, the coach has made a conscious effort to shield their family’s affairs from the public eye.

Pete Carroll and Glena Goranson have 3 children together

After graduating from USC with a degree in Psychology, Nate received a job offer from his father to join the Seattle Seahawks after completing his education. Since then, he has been a consistent presence with the team, notably contributing to the Super Bowl victory alongside his father.

Pete Carroll excelled as a multisport athlete in high school

Carroll was born on September 15, 1951, in San Francisco, California, to Rita and James Edward. His paternal great-grandparents were Irish immigrants, while his Croatian maternal grandparents emigrated from the Šibenik region. 

Raised in Greenbrae, California, he attended Greenbrae School and later went on to Redwood High School in Larkspur, California. Carroll excelled as a multi-sport athlete, participating in football (as a quarterback, wide receiver, and defensive back), basketball, and baseball. 

His outstanding performance earned him the title of Athlete of the Year during his senior year in 1969, as mentioned by the LA Times. In recognition of his achievements, he was inducted into the charter class of the Redwood High School Athletic Hall of Fame in April 2009.

Following high school, Carroll began his college journey at the College of Marin, a junior college, where he played football for two years. He then transferred to the University of the Pacific, where he was a member of the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity. 

During his time at Pacific, Carroll played free safety for the Tigers, receiving All-Pacific Coast Athletic Association honors in both 1971 and 1972. In 1973, he earned his Bachelor of Science in Business Administration.

Fast-forward to the present, Carroll stands as one of three head coaches to achieve both a college football national championship and an NFL championship.
